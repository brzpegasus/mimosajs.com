Optimization Explained zip it up, flip it, compress it down

In the normal course of development you want to have your assets loaded individually rather than in one merged file. And you certainly don't want your assets minified or mangled. This all makes for easier debugging.

But when you take your application to production, you want to include all the performance improvements that come with merging, optimizing, and minifying your assets. For both the build and watch commands, Mimosa provides --optimize and --minify flags that combine to provide you both the ability to fully optimize your application, and to have a bit of control over what gets optimized.

Mimosa uses the r.js optimizer which is perfect for optimizing RequireJS applications. Underneath the hood, that optimizer is using Uglify to shrink your codebase down as tiny as possible. Mimosa also comes bundled with Uglify itself, and can use it to individually shrink your assets. And Mimosa's Uglify and the r.js optimizer can be combined to provide more fine-grained control of your minification strategy.
